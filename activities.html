Club Activities

Our club organizes various activities where members can participate and learn!

  • Weekly Greenhouse Sessions

    Students come to learn about plant propagation and general plant maintenance by cutting back and repotting plants, learning plant identification, identifying damaged plants, and propagating cuttings of plants on a weekly basis.

  • Gourd Night Light Making at Gourdlandia (Once a Semester)

    Gourdlandia Field Trip: Students helped sort and send out various gourd seedlings, then got a tour of the gourd operation before carving and decorating miniature gourd nightlights to take home.

  • Tillandsia Mounting Event

    Educated people on air plant care and maintenance, then taught 45 people to mount air plants on bark media.

  • Field Trips to Local Businesses

    Students traveled to the carnivorous plant greenhouse operation of Florae collaborative, and got insight into different carnivorous plant care, how the greenhouses are maintained and how controlled environmental agriculture (CEA) facilities work. In addition, they learned about how plants can be micropropagated.

  • Annual Participation in the Philadelphia Flower Show

    Every year, our club competes in the Philadelphia Flower Show, showcasing our expertise and passion for plants. In the last competition:

    • First Prize in Class 4176

      We won first prize in “Class 4176: Pubescent (woolly or hairy) cactus or other succulent plant except Mammillaria. Pot: 6" or under.” for our Ariocarpus retusus spp.

    • First Prize in Class 4212

      We also secured the first prize in “Class 4212: Any species Begonia (no hybrids or cultivars), flowering or foliage.” for our Begonia melanobullata.

    • First Prize in Class 4206

      Lastly, our club took home the first prize in “Class 4206: Begonia, rhizomatous, informal foliage.” for our Begonia 'Nebula Zeta'.
